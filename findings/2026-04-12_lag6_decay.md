# Lag-6 Reversal Effect Is Decaying — 2026-04-12 (round 4)

**The most important finding of this research program so far.** The cross-asset 24h reversal is a real historical pattern that has been progressively arbitraged away.

Effect size halved from 2020 → 2021 and has continued to decay. 2024 bump (r=-0.08) is plausibly the post-FTX / banking-crisis dislocation temporarily widening the window before algos readapted. By 2025-2026 the effect is statistically indistinguishable from zero.

## Why this matters more than a tradeable signal

1. **It's a direct, quantitative observation of market efficiency in crypto.** We have a rare empirical measurement of an inefficiency's half-life. The 24h cross-asset reversal was stable for 4 years (2017-2020), compressed by half for 3 years (2021-2023), and is gone in year 9+.

2. **It explains why continuous-regression and ensemble extraction failed.** Testing a single β across 2017-2026 assumes stationarity. The true β is time-varying with large magnitude in early folds and ~zero late.  The aggregate correlation survives because early years dominate, but any trading signal built on the full-sample β is betting on a world that no longer exists.

3. **It structurally rules out a whole class of hypotheses.** Any "cross-asset reversal at N-hour horizon" strategy should be expected to face the same decay. The arbitrage pressure that killed lag-6 would kill lag-4, lag-8, etc. likewise.

## What this implies for forward research

**Stop looking for inefficiencies in dimensions where pro arbitrageurs have had years to dig.** Public-data lag effects on major liquid pairs are a picked-over search space.

Higher-EV directions are those where arbitrage pressure is structurally weaker:

1. **Event-conditional effects** — patterns that only fire in specific regimes (e.g., liquidation cascades, funding reset events, exchange-specific outages). These don't have stable features to arbitrage against continuously.

2. **Cross-venue microstructure** — same asset, different exchanges, orderbook imbalance / funding divergence. Requires venue-specific data we don't have. This is an infrastructure roadmap item, not a hypothesis.

3. **Behavioral / narrative regimes** — retail-driven vs institutional-driven market phases have different statistical signatures. On-chain flow composition (large vs small wallets) may identify regime *before* price reveals it. We have on-chain volume but not wallet-tier breakdown — needs a new data source (Glassnode/Dune).

4. **Newer asset pairs** — SOL data starts 2022, so the lag-6 result there (r=-0.045 pooled) sits in the post-decay era. If anything is there, it's smaller but maybe hasn't hit zero yet. Expand to newer majors (e.g., if TAO/USDT, TIA/USDT have sufficient history) where the arb capital hasn't saturated.

## Meta-finding about the research program

The system's design (refuse to promote sub-significance, require OOS walk-forward, record negatives) produced a higher-value finding by NOT discovering a strategy. If we had used fold 3 alone (Sharpe +2.25) we would have promoted a dead signal. The walk-forward structure forced us to see the temporal dimension, which led to the decay analysis, which is the genuinely durable knowledge.

**Update to research strategy:** add a "stationarity check" step to the methodology — for any new statistical pattern, verify the effect is stable across temporal sub-samples before building a signal. A pooled significance test without stability is a trap.
